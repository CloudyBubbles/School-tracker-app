# Design North Star — the living journal

The single reference for every design/cosmetic decision. When a choice is unclear, it should serve this.

## The feeling (William's words)
A leather-back journal that's **heavy the more you put in it**. Looks heavy and worn — like it was **passed down to you**. But inside: a **new coat of paint**. It smells like **you and the work you do**. Reference vibe: **The Last of Us** (worn, weathered, real) and the **Metro Exodus** journal (a personal artifact carried through everything).

The one-line test: **worn outside, alive inside, and it remembers you.**

## Right now it's dead. The goal is alive.
"Alive" = it reacts to you. It changes because of what you do, not on a timer. It should feel like it has a pulse — small, earned responses, never gimmicky.

## Principles that flow from this
1. **The outside ages; the inside stays crisp.** Cover/spine/edges accumulate wear with use. The pages you write on stay clean and legible — the "new coat of paint." Never sacrifice readability for texture.
2. **Weight = accumulation.** The more real work has flowed through it, the heavier and thicker it looks. Wear is *earned*, tied to what you've actually done — not decorative noise added from nothing.
3. **It remembers you.** Subtle marks of your own patterns — your busiest subject, your late-night sessions — make it feel personal, like it's yours and no one else's.
4. **Reactive, not scripted.** Delight comes from responses to *your* actions (finishing, focusing, falling behind), not idle animation.
5. **Restraint.** TLOU/Metro feel real because they're understated. One perfect worn detail beats ten. If it feels like a theme skin, it's wrong.

## The signature — "The Living Volume" (candidate jaw-drop)
The book physically **thickens and wears with everything you've ever put through it** — spine softening, corners rounding, edges darkening, page-block growing. At year's (or term's) end it **closes, ages a final notch, and takes its place on the bookshelf** as a finished, worn volume you can pull back out and flip through. A fresh, crisp volume opens for the new year. Over time: a **shelf of your own worn volumes** — a tactile record of everything you've done.

Why it sets you apart: no schoolwork tracker is a *heirloom that ages with you*. It's the difference between "an app that stores tasks" and "the thing on my shelf that holds my year." This is the one to build toward — everything on the cosmetic backlog (aging leather, new-volume ceremony, bookshelf) is a piece of it.

## Portfolio angle
This is also the story: "I started knowing nothing and built a living, aging journal from scratch." The signature is what makes that story land in a demo — it's the jaw-drop moment for a prospect, not just for you.
